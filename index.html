About

I’m a physicist and PhD candidate at the University of Barcelona (IN2UB, MIND Group), working on integrated photonics, optomechanics, and plasmonic biosensing. My current research explores strategies to optimize and combine plasmonic–polariton and cavity optomechanical sensors for multiparameter sensing.

I work across the full pipeline, from device design and simulation (COMSOL) to optical characterization and data analysis (Python/MATLAB). My projects include experimental studies of 1D optomechanical nanocavities and the development and testing of miniaturized SPR platforms. Previously, I worked at ICN2 as a Research Support Technician in the NanoB2A group, contributing to plasmonic devices for real-time biomarker detection and to automation workflows (LabVIEW/Python).

Alongside research, I have teaching and academic service experience, including laboratory instruction in basic digital design (VHDL + ModelSim) and participation in the PAU Physics exam boards (invigilation and marking).
